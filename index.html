Experience

Johnson & Johnson

Application Support Analyst

One week after earning my Salesforce Admin certification, I was grateful to land a job with Johnson & Johnson as an Application Support Analyst. Technology Services within J&J can be split up into two sectors: BUILD and RUN. The RUN sector can be split up even further into various clusters with varying technology specialties. The cluster I serve on is called Customer & Sales which specializes primarily in Salesforce technology. Our application portfolio boasts over 30 unique Salesforce instances. Each of these Salesforce applications are owned by a business, and the day-to-day support of these applications is contracted to a 3rd-party vendor (Cognizant for Customer & Sales). Thus, the RUN team serves as a liaison between the owning-business and the vendor.

With the support of an application contracted, I seldomly find myself within a Salesforce instance doing actual support. However, I am helping triage Incident tickets submitted by the application users through an internal ticketing tool developed by J&J called IRIS. Thus, my understanding of Salesforce is being applied and growing every day to help mitigate issues.

On top of supporting exisiting applications, my responsibilities also include on-boarding new applications built by the BUILD team over to the support team members. This process includes enabling the application for support within the IRIS ticketing tool, and educating the owning-business members on said ticketing tool and the general application maintenance support model of J&J. I also ensure that the support team is well-equipped to support a new application which is done through knowledge transfer, shadowing, reverse shadowing, and documentation. Such key documentation includes a Technical Design Specifications (TDS) and an Operations Run Book (ORB). A TDS is created by the BUILD team during development, and details the entire inner workings of an application. An ORB further details information that may not be covered by a TDS such as points of failures, impacted user communities, communication and resolution protocols for outages, and comprehensive contact information.

While that covers most of my day-to-day duties, I am also responsible for several long-term projects:

  • Operations Run Book (ORB): When I came onto the team and started learning about the cluster's applications, I was pointed towards the application's corresponding ORB to learn more about supporting said application. However, we soon realized the overall landscape of our cluster's ORBs was in a poor state. Thus, I was put in charge of an effort to work with the various support teams to help improve the quality of each application's ORB and bring them to our baseline standards.
  • Aggrify: One of my favorite projects was enabling a service-level targeting tool offered by another RUN team cluster (Data & Analytics) called Aggrify. This tool captures pertinent metadata about any jobs your application may have. We enabled this tool specifically for Scheduled Apex classes within our various Salesforce applications. With Aggrify enabled, we can monitor for job success and failures, analyze the mean time for a job to complete, and troubleshoot possible solutions to make the job run more efficiently.
  • Knowledge Articles: Whenever a Incident ticket is resolved in IRIS, you are required to find or create an appropriate Knowledge Article that details the steps taken to resolve the issue in order to close the ticket. Over-time you build a strong Knowledge Base, and steps to resolve repeated issues can be found easily. However, over-time we have found that the vendor has cut corners, and they create lazy Knowledge Articles that have no sustenance whatsoever, and only pollute the Knowledge Base. To help mitigate this issue, our cluster created an audit process to randomly select various Knowledge Articles created in the prior month and verify the quality of their content. If a Knowledge Article does not meet the baseline standards, we provide feedback to the original author and they are to update it appropriately.

Franco Signor

Intern Junior Developer

Jun 2015 – Aug 2018

While attending college at University of South Florida Sarasota-Manatee, I was fortunate enough to intern part-time as a Junior Developer at Franco Signor LLC. The company's operations deal within the Medicare industry, more specifically Medicare Secondary Payer (MSP) compliance and solutions. As a Junior Developer, my day-to-day responsibilities included:

  • Program numerous SSIS packages that query client data using SQL to then transform and import into the appropriate Salesforce objects.
  • Design and develop various internal, web-based applications (.NET/C#) that allow the sales and operations teams to interact with client data.
  • Collaborate in a team environment with IT, operations, and sales to design and create customized solutions crucial to business operations.
  • Maintain existing applications to eliminate bugs, add minor enhancements, and improve business productivity.
  • Develop customized SSRS reports for analysis based on given requirements.
  • Database design, maintenance, and normalization in SQL Server Management Studio
  • Provide maintenance and support for company’s public facing WordPress website (https://francosignor.com/).
